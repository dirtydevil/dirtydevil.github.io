I am always interested in prototyping ideas by keeping aesthetic design in mind.

Currently pursuing Masters of Human Interface Technology (MHIT) at HITLab NZ in University of Canterbury, Christchurch, NZ.

Before that I was working on intelligent wearable Shoes/insoles that helps visually challenged to navigate from one place to other. I have also worked on innovative medical embedded devices which helped diabetics to track their glucose readings.

Earlier, I attended conferences and workshops, have worked on many small projects, collaborated in some and tried lots of stuff related to Electronics, Arduino, Sensors, Gesture Recognition, Serial Communication, Computer Vision, Kinect, Robotics and more. During under-graduation, I was an active member of LUGB (Linux User Group Bikaner) and organized many FOSS events in Techfests, Software freedom day, lots of Linux installfests, workshops related to "Arduino for beginners", "Basic tutorial of Linux" etc.
